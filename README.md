# Global Oil Price Analysis (2015вЂ“2025): Strategic Insights for Pertamina 2026

This project analyzes global Brent crude oil price trends from **2015 to 2025** to generate strategic insights for **Pertamina's potential business strategy in 2026**.

The analysis focuses on identifying market trends, trading activity, and price volatility in the global oil market. The final results are presented through an interactive **Tableau dashboard**.

---

## Project Overview

Global oil prices are influenced by geopolitical events, economic cycles, and supply-demand dynamics. Understanding historical patterns can help energy companies make better strategic decisions.

This project aims to:

вЂў Analyze long-term Brent crude oil price trends
вЂў Identify periods of extreme market volatility
вЂў Examine trading activity in the oil market
вЂў Generate strategic implications for energy companies such as **Pertamina**

---

## Dashboard Preview

The final dashboard contains three main analytical views:

1. **Global Oil Price Trend (2015вЂ“2025)**  
   Shows Brent crude oil prices along with **30-day and 90-day moving averages** to highlight short-term and long-term trends.

2. **Global Oil Market Trading Volume**  
   Visualizes trading activity in the global oil market to identify periods of increased market participation.

3. **Monthly Oil Price Volatility**  
   Displays price fluctuations based on period-to-period price changes to highlight unstable market periods.

## Key Insights

вЂў Oil prices experienced extreme volatility during the COVID-19 shock in 2020.
вЂў The global energy crisis in 2022 drove oil prices to their highest level in a decade.

## Strategic Implications for Pertamina (2026)

Based on the analysis, several strategic considerations emerge:

вЂў Maintain **flexible crude procurement strategies** during high volatility periods.
вЂў Consider **hedging strategies** when oil prices approach historical resistance levels.
вЂў Increased trading activity suggests stronger **speculative pressure** in global oil markets.
вЂў Diversification of crude supply sources can help reduce **exposure to geopolitical risks**.
